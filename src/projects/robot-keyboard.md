I designed this robot for a set of neuroscience experiments investigating passive learning of finger movements through robotic assistance. Passive movement of one hand is generated by mirroring active movement of the other hand. I implemented predictive control to make movements between both hands feel perceptually similar, as mismatches were detected even with small delays (~25 milliseconds). I'm also quite proud of the mechanical design and ergonomics of the device.

- **predictive control** removes perceptual lag during mirroring
- switch between **active and passive movement** on the fly
- **virtual spring** for naturalistic typing movements
- **simple adjustment** for all hand and finger sizes
